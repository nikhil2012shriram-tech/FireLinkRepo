## 1. Introduction

FireLink is a wildfire readiness and action app to help homeowners proactively prepare for wildfires and protect their homes and families.

- Wildfire risk is rising rapidly due to climate change, urban expansion, and drought conditions
- Over 1,100 communities across 32 states face significant wildfire risk today (headwaterseconomics.org)
- Homeowners increasingly lack tools to translate risk into clear, actionable steps
- Shift from reactive alerts → proactive preparedness is a major behavioral gap
- FireLink positions itself as the "operating system" for homeowner wildfire readiness

---

## 2. Problem

Users today struggle with understanding their actual wildfire risk, knowing what actions matter most, and consistently preparing before wildfire season becomes dangerous.

- 1.2M+ homes in the U.S. face very high wildfire risk, with massive exposure to loss (valuepenguin.com)
- Risk data is fragmented—maps, alerts, insurance insights are not integrated into one view
- FEMA and traditional models often misclassify risk at the property level (vox.com)
- Homeowners rely on static checklists → no prioritization or ongoing guidance
- Result: low action rates despite high awareness (awareness ≠ behavior change)

---

## 3. What We Offer (Solution)

FireLink is a personalized wildfire preparedness app that helps users assess their risk, prioritize prevention tasks, and stay ready year-round. What makes us different is that we turn complex wildfire data into a simple, guided action plan tailored to each home.

- Property-level risk scoring using environmental + structural signals
- Dynamic task engine that prioritizes highest-impact mitigation actions
- Continuous readiness model (weekly tasks + high-risk day alerts)
- Household preparedness layer (family plans, evacuation readiness)
- **Differentiation:** translates data → behavior, not just insight

---

## 4. Product Validation

Evidence shows strong unmet demand for proactive, personalized wildfire preparedness tools among homeowners.

- **Insurance market signal:** major insurers (e.g., State Farm, Allstate) have reduced or exited coverage in high-risk states like California → homeowners actively seeking ways to reduce risk and maintain insurability
- **Behavior gap:** studies show most homeowners are aware of wildfire risk, but fewer than 30–40% complete key mitigation actions (defensible space, home hardening)
- **Government push:** agencies like CAL FIRE and FEMA emphasize homeowner responsibility, but provide static guidance → gap in execution and prioritization
- **Rising personal cost:** average wildfire-related property losses and premiums are increasing sharply, creating financial motivation to act earlier
- **Existing tools failure:** alert apps and checklists have high awareness but low engagement/retention → indicates need for guided, ongoing experience

---

## 5. App Features

Here's how it works: users input their home and property details, receive a personalized risk score and prioritized action plan, and get weekly tasks and real-time readiness updates. This turns overwhelming wildfire information into clear, actionable steps homeowners can actually follow.

1. **Step 1:** Property assessment (location, vegetation, structure)
2. **Step 2:** Risk scoring + prioritized mitigation plan
3. **Step 3:** Weekly checklist + seasonal readiness tracking
4. Clear, personalized "what to do this week" guidance

**Outcome:** moves user from awareness → consistent action

This is a large and growing market driven by global warming causing increasing wildfire risk in residential areas. We'll start with a specific wildfire-prone city or county in the western United States and grow to regions and states.

- U.S. wildfire costs estimated at $394B–$893B annually (economic impact) (jec.senate.gov)
- ~$500B+ potential rebuild exposure from high-risk homes (valuepenguin.com)
- 1.2M+ high-risk homes (core initial SAM)
- TAM expands to broader climate risk/home resilience category
- **Initial SOM:** high-risk Western U.S. homeowners (CA, CO, TX, AZ)

## 9. Competition

Current solutions exist, but none provide an end-to-end, action-oriented preparedness system. FireLink is better because it provides a personalized, proactive, and continuous guidance system rather than reactive alerts or static advice.

| Competitor Type | Examples | Gap |
|----------------|----------|-----|
| Alert & Mapping Apps | Watch Duty, FEMA App, PulsePoint | Purely reactive; only useful once risk is already high |
| Government & Agency Tools | CAL FIRE, Ready Set Go Program | Static, generic; not personalized or behavior-driven |
| Risk Data & Analytics | ZestyAI, CoreLogic, First Street Foundation | Not consumer-friendly; don't translate insights into actions |
| Insurance & Home Services | State Farm, Allstate | Influence behavior through pricing, but don't guide risk reduction |
| DIY / Status Quo | Google, PDFs, or doing nothing | High friction, low follow-through, no prioritization |

**FireLink Advantage:** Combines risk scoring, prioritization, weekly actions, and behavior loops — shifts homeowners from awareness to execution, which no current solution solves.
